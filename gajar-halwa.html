Gajar Halwa

A classic North Indian dessert made from grated carrots slow-cooked with milk and sugar.

Prep Time: 1.5-2 Hours | Difficulty: Medium

Ingredients You'll Need 🛒:

  • 1 kg Carrots (Gajar), peeled and grated
  • 1 liter Full-fat Milk
  • 1 cup Sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 4 tbsp Ghee (Clarified Butter)
  • 1/2 tsp Cardamom powder
  • Handful of Cashews and Raisins
  • 2 tbsp Khoya (optional, for richness)

Step-by-Step Cooking Instructions 👨‍🍳:

  1. Sauté: Heat 1 tbsp ghee in a heavy-bottomed pan. Add the grated carrots and sauté for 5 minutes until they soften slightly.
  2. Cook with Milk: Pour in the milk and bring to a boil. Simmer on medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the milk evaporates completely.
  3. Sweeten: Add sugar and the remaining ghee. The halwa will turn liquidy again as the sugar melts.
  4. Thicken: Continue cooking and stirring until the mixture thickens and starts leaving the sides of the pan.
  5. Flavor: Stir in the cardamom powder, nuts, and raisins. If using khoya, crumble it in now for extra creaminess.
  6. Serve hot or warm, perfect for winter evenings!
